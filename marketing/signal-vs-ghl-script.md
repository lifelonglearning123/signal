# Sales video script — Signal vs GHL Voice AI (voice + margin angle, ~60 sec)

**Audience:** GHL agency owners who already know / use GHL's built-in Voice AI.
**Wedge:** the two most defensible edges — it sounds human (their #1 complaint) + it's margin, not a per-sub-account cost.
**Verify before publishing:** the complaint counts (▲178 / ▲174) and the $97/sub-account figure — sources in `signal-vs-ghl-battlecard.md`.

---

## ▶️ Primary script (~60 sec)

| # | On-screen / visual | Voiceover |
|---|---|---|
| 1 · Hook (0–6s) | Two waveforms side by side; label flips: **"Robotic" → "Human"**. | "You already have Voice AI in GoHighLevel. So why are agencies ripping it out?" |
| 2 · Problem (6–18s) | GHL feature-request board: *"robotic voice — 178 votes."* A caller frowning, hanging up. | "Two reasons. One — the number-one complaint about GHL's voice is that it sounds like a robot. And when your client's customer hears a robot, they hang up and call the next business." |
| 3 · Cost (18–28s) | Calculator: **$97 × 10 sub-accounts = $970/mo**. Money leaving. | "Two — it costs you ninety-seven dollars a month, *per sub-account*. Ten clients? That's nearly a thousand a month — a cost buried inside your stack that you can't really mark up." |
| 4 · Shift (28–40s) | Signal call playing — natural voice, booking a slot. Your logo on the dashboard. | "Signal fixes both. It's built on premium voices that sound completely human, and it's *your* product — white-label, on your domain — for a hundred and forty-nine dollars a month. For *unlimited* clients." |
| 5 · Proof/benefit (40–52s) | Split: Signal column lights up — human voice · pick the AI brain · caller synced to CRM · silence/drop alerts. | "You pick a smarter brain for your best clients. Every call's name and summary lands back in the GHL contact. And you get an alert the second a bot goes quiet — so you fix it before your client ever notices." |
| 6 · Offer + CTA (52–60s) | End card: **$149/mo · unlimited clients · you set the price**. CTA. | "Same job GHL does — answer and book. Except this one sounds human, and *you* set what your clients pay. Book a 20-minute walkthrough and hear the difference." |

---

## 🎣 Alternative hooks
1. **Blunt:** "GHL's Voice AI sounds like a robot. That's costing your clients customers."
2. **Money:** "You're paying $97 a client to sound robotic. There's a better way."
3. **Insider:** "Every GHL agency has Voice AI. Almost none of them are making money on it."

## ▶️ 30-second cut
**(0–4s)** "You've got Voice AI in GHL. Here's why agencies are switching."
**(4–14s)** "GHL's #1 voice complaint? It sounds robotic — and callers hang up. And it's $97 per sub-account, so 10 clients is nearly a grand a month of cost."
**(14–24s)** "Signal sounds completely human, it's white-label under your brand, and it's $149 for *unlimited* clients — you set what each one pays."
**(24–30s)** "Same job. Human voice. Your margin. Book a walkthrough 👇"

## 🎬 Production notes
- **Show the contrast literally:** play a stiff robotic clip vs a Signal call. The audio A/B is the whole pitch.
- Keep the $970 vs $149 frame on screen — the economics do the closing.
- Captions on (muted autoplay). Use real UI for the dashboard beat; don't let AI hallucinate the product.
- CTA variants: paid ad → "Book a walkthrough"; organic → "Comment DEMO and I'll send you a side-by-side"; `/agencies` page → Deploy.
- Honesty guardrail: don't claim GHL *can't* use ElevenLabs (it can, at a premium) — the pitch is human-by-default + the economics, not "GHL has no good voices."
